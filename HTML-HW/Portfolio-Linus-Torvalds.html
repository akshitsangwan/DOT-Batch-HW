Linus Torvalds

The Creator of Linux

Biography

Linus Torvalds is a Finnish-American software engineer who is the creator and principal developer of the Linux kernel, which is the kernel for a number of operating syst IEEEems. He also create the version control system Git. Torvalds was born on December 28, 1969 in Helsinki, Finland. He studied computer science at the University of Helsinki, and he developed the Linux kernal as a student project in 1961. Since then, the Linus kernel has become one of the most widely used kernels in the world, powering everything from smartphones to supercomputers.

Achievements

  • 1991 - Create the Linus kernel and the version control system Git
  • 2004 - Named one of the"100 Most Influential People in the World"by Time magazine in 2004
  • 2010 - Received an honorary doctorate from the University of Helsinki in 2010
  • 2010 - Received the 2010 Millennium Technology Prize and the 2012 Free Software Foundation's Award for the Advancement of Free Software
  • 2013 - Inducted into the Hall of Fame in 2013 for his contributions to the development of the Linux kernel and the creation of Git
  • 2014 - Inducted into the Hall of Fame of the Computer Pioneer Award
  • 2015 - Received the 2015 IEEE Computer Society Computer Pioneer Award
  • 2019 - Received the 2019 ACM SIGOPS Hall of Fame Award
  • 2021 - Awarded the Takeda Foundation's 2021 Takeda Award for Social Contribution, which recognizes individuals who have made significant contributions to the field of consumer electronics
